Form Leads — Quoted + Cubic Feet

Each row in the Granot Follow Up Estimates table is matched to a Vantage form lead by ref_no (the Mongo ID). For every match the popup tells you exactly what will happen: lead with a booking attached (idempotent sync), lead with no booking that already matches (idempotent sync), or lead with no booking where quoted or cubic_feet will change. Sync individually, select-and-sync, or schedule via ScanAndSync.

How matches are decided & what the badges mean (click to expand)

We look up every row in Vantage by ref_no = Mongo _id of a form lead.

  • found · has booking — Vantage form lead has an attached BookedLead. Sync still runs idempotently so you can re-assert quoted and cubic_feet; nothing else changes.
  • found · idempotent — Form lead has no booking and quoted + cubic_feet already match the Granot row. Sync sends the same values back to Vantage.
  • found · will update — Form lead has no booking and sync will change at least one of quoted or cubic_feet. The exact diff is shown when you expand the row.
  • invalid ref_no — The ref_no column is not a valid Mongo ID (typically "not provided" or empty).
  • unsupported prior — The prior column is not 0 or 1 (e.g. booked at prior 5). Sync is not run automatically.

Every interval, ScanAndSync re-runs the same logic as clicking Sync All Supported. Errors are reported in the cycle history below and do not stop the loop.

Call Leads — Match by Phone or Job No

Granot's Follow Up Estimates and Booked Jobs tables are reconciled into Vantage call leads using the same matching rules: first by phone_number, then by job_no. The popup tells you which method matched, whether the call lead already has a Vantage booking attached, and exactly which fields would update. Sync always runs even when no fields change (idempotent).

Auto-syncing

Actions

ScanAndSync (auto loop)

How matches are decided & what the badges mean (click to expand)

Each Granot row is reconciled against Vantage call leads in this order:

  1. By phone_number: normalize the phone and look up call leads with the same number.
  2. By job_no: when phone matching does not find anything, fall back to job_no (the Granot job_no column on Booked Jobs and Follow Up Estimates).

What the badges and statuses mean:

  • by phone — Vantage matched on phone only. Either no job_no was on the call lead yet, or the job_no on the call lead is different — the popup will show which fields update on sync.
  • by job_no — Vantage matched purely on job_no (typical for Booked Jobs rows).
  • by phone + job_no — Both identifiers pointed at the same call lead. Highest-confidence match.
  • has booking — The matched call lead already has a Vantage booking attached. Sync is still allowed and runs idempotently — fields are refreshed but the booking link is preserved.
  • not found — Neither phone nor job_no matched any call lead. The customer's phone number may have been changed in Granot or the lead is older than Vantage's retention window.

Follow Up Estimates and Booked Jobs share the same matching rules; they are shown as separate tables for clarity. The Update Booked Call Leads button syncs the Booked Jobs rows in bulk; Sync All Supported syncs the Follow Up Estimates rows.
